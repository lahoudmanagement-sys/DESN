CHICXULUB

/CHEEK–shoo–loob/  ·  N.  ·  THE METEOR THAT ENDED THE DINOSAURS

66 million years ago, a meteor struck the Yucatán and made the dominant species of its era extinct. We named our wing after it — because we're doing the same thing to rigid aviation.

THE DEVELOPMENT

From fishbone to flight.

Every breakthrough has a backstory. Here's ours — the baseline we set out to beat, and the prototypes that got us there.

FIG · 02 / BASELINE fishbone wing
reference photo
01 · BASELINE + GOAL

Improve the fishbone.

Presented with a standard fish-bone model, we aimed to improve on its failings — namely the reliability and longevity of the design's high-stress bending motion. By disruptively innovating upon this design, we hoped to achieve a performance peak equal to that of a perfect NACA 2415 profile in the wind tunnel and better than a traditional flap wing.

FIG · 03 / PROTOTYPES prototype iteration
photo
02 · PROTOTYPES

Iterate. Streamline. Lift.

Prototypes explored manufacturing, actuation and overall design choices. Iteration allowed us to streamline manufacturing and modularity of the system, form an actuation system that sat within the performant 2415 profile, and improve the morphing capability of the wing whilst drastically reducing weight.

THE HYBRID ARCHITECTURE

An industry-first: mechanical meets biological.

Every morphing wing before ours failed the same way — stress, fatigue, unreliable return-to-shape. Our hybrid architecture solves all three.

01 / MECHANICAL AXLES
Precision movement.
A series of rigid rotational axles drive the wing's morphing action with zero actuation-lag and full control authority.
02 / BIO-INSPIRED SPINE
Natural flex.
A flexible vertebral spine distributes aerodynamic load the way a fish distributes water pressure — smoothly, and across the full length of the wing.
03 / HYBRID ADVANTAGE
Reliability solved.
Fishbone wings stress the whole structure during morph. Our axle-spine system isolates stress to rotation points — and guarantees return to optimal cruise geometry, every cycle.
